Timeline

Our research pods examine a new title every 2 weeks. The Immersive Design Pattern Study will run for a total of 36 weeks, with each of 4 pods evaluating 18 titles. Research results will be published in early 2022.  Building the study was an iterative and collaborative process that happened over many months.

Final Publications | Spring 2022

  • Best practices white paper for interaction design in VR (and other correlations discovered)
  • Catalogue of evaluation findings and summaries
  • A framework for evaluating interaction patterns in VR
  • Knowledge base for VR and user experience terminology

Final Analysis | Fall 2021


Test Launch | Spring 2021

  • One title is evaluated by each pod every two weeks.
  • Analysts summarize the resulting data from each pod and prepare it for final analysis.
  • Team members reach out to creators and producers to procure titles for evaluation.

Beta Test | Winter 2021

  • Testing results and feedback are used to improve the evaluation.
  • A fifth evaluator is added to each pod to provide a buffer for steady data collection.
  • A second analyst is added to each pod to balance workload.
  • VEIL team grows to 32.
  • A functional review of the revised evaluation form helps to further polish the test and onboard new team members.

Functional QA Test | Fall 2020

  • A dynamic evaluation form is built in Qualtrics to cover the broad range of possible interaction patterns in VR.
  • Each team member pilots the test with a title of their choosing.
  • VEIL team grows to 22.

Results from Alpha Test Analysis | Summer 2020

The current process of interaction evaluation is not sufficient for the wide range of experiences being evaluated. Committees are created to:

  • Establish an evaluation framework for interactions in VR that is both structured and flexible.
  • Build a team and data structure for summarizing the evaluation results.
  • Add usability studies for accessibility to the test plan; an accessibility consultant is also added to the team.

Alpha Test | Spring 2020

  • A pilot study is launched to test the evaluation form, pod structure, and team communication protocols.
  • Each pod evaluates one title in each of three categories, testing all 12 categories across four pods.
  • VEIL grows to 16 volunteers.

Personas and Test Cases | Winter 2020

‍To ensure each evaluator is collecting comparable data and that foundational assumptions about use cases are consistent for each title:

  • A persona is created for each use case category.
  • A template is established for creating a test guide for each title.

Proof of Concept | Fall 2019

  • First test protocol is created as a web form.
  • Alpha test run in Sketchbox 3D.VEIL grows to six volunteer members.

Initial Idea | Early 2019

‍Founders Rob Dongas and Suzan Oslin decide to embark on a project to evaluate interaction design patterns in VR.

Committees Created

To address re-architecting the evaluation form and the evaluation summaries, committees are created. Accessibility usability studies are added to the test plan.
